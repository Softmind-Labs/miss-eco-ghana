Where Development Meets Elegance

Miss Eco Ghana is a prestigious national pageant dedicated to celebrating beauty with purpose, environmental stewardship, and youth leadership. Over the course of the competition, inspiring young Ghanaian women come together to champion sustainability, climate action, and social impact while showcasing confidence, intelligence, and cultural pride.

Meet the Queens of 2026

Miss Eco Ghana 2026 Winner

Michelle Appeah

Michelle Appeah is a Ghanaian, born in London and currently based between Japan, the UK, and Ghana. She works in sustainability, connecting investors with nature restoration projects, and serves as an African Champion for the ONE Campaign as well as an Advisory Council member of Solar Sister — supporting clean energy and poverty-focused advocacy across Africa.

Alongside this, she is a model and artist who has worked with fashion brands and exhibited her artwork in Tokyo. As Miss Eco Ghana 2026, Michelle will represent Ghana in Miss Eco International in May, amplifying environmental advocacy and promoting a more sustainable and prosperous world.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I register?

To register, click Register in the tab, type in your details and click send. You will receive a confirmation email and a scheduled screening time to begin your journey.

How much does registration cost?

Registration costs 200 GH₵ for Ghanaians and $50 for Ghanaians living abroad.

Will the auditions be physical?

Yes, there will be a physical audition after you have crossed the first stage, which is the online screening stage.

Will I be evicted during the online screening stage?

The online screening stage is for stakeholders and judges to connect better with contestants, hence there is no eviction during the screening process.

How long will the competition last?

The competition lasts about 3 months depending on the activities involved.

Would there be camping or physical activities?

Yes, there will be physical activities like catwalk training, grooming, and physical tasks. Camping will only be in the last week of the Grand Finale.

What do I need to do as a finalist of Miss Eco Ghana?

As a finalist, you need to be passionate about environmental issues and seek to understand and propose better solutions for solving them. You need to be proactive with tasks and seek to achieve the very best throughout the competition.
